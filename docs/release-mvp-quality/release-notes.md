# Release notes — MVP Quality (draft)

_For the public changelog. Keep it warm and brief; no engineering jargon._

---

### A quieter, steadier app

This release focuses on the small things that make My Quiet Space feel
more trustworthy and easier to return to. Nothing new and loud — just less
friction, more calm.

**What's better**

- **InnerMate is gentler to start with.** A cleaner empty state, simpler
  composer, and clearer follow-up suggestions after each reply, so it's
  easier to find your way back in.
- **History is easier to reach on the phone.** A new drawer keeps your past
  conversations one tap away without crowding the screen.
- **Your weekly letter says what it means.** We rewrote a few small lines
  in Home and Settings so the letter's tone and timing match what you
  actually receive.
- **Reminders are honest about what they do.** If a reminder runs only
  inside the browser tab, we now say so.
- **Mood arcs read correctly.** The companion now uses the same 1-5 scale
  you see in check-ins.

**Under the hood**

- Accessibility pass across screens (focus, labels, contrast, reduced motion).
- Calm protection on the InnerMate and Weekly Letter assistants so the app
  stays responsive even if something goes wrong upstream.
- A private record of which assistant version produced which reply, so we
  can keep improving the tone safely.
- The first steps of multi-language support — your words still stay yours.

**Unchanged**

- Your data is still private. Memories and journal entries are not shared
  with the assistant unless you mark them readable.
- Safety paths still take priority — if you ever type something heavy, the
  app responds with care and resources first.

_Thank you for being here, quietly._
